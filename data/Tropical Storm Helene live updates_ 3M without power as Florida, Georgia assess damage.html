Live updates Millions without power as Helene treks north, spurring Carolina floods and heavy winds

clock iconUpdated
September 27, 2024 at 11:56 a.m. EDT
Listen
1 min
Share
Save

More than 3 million customers in Florida, Georgia and the Carolinas were without electricity Friday morning as Helene sped north, leaving a deadly and destructive trail after striking Florida’s Big Bend area the night before. The Weather Service has warned of “life-threatening” flooding in North Carolina, where several more inches could fall Friday. At least seven people have been confirmed killed in Florida, Georgia and North Carolina as the system, now a tropical storm, came ashore as a Category 4 hurricane. Catastrophic winds and torrential rain from the storm were expected to continue Friday across the Southeast and southern Appalachians.

Key updates
  • Widespread ‘catastrophic’ flooding unfolding in the western Carolinas
  • More than 3 million customers without power in Florida, Georgia and Carolinas
  • Rain totals from Helene top a foot from Florida to the Carolinas, setting records
